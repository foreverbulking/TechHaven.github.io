Our Vision

Your Trusted Marketplace for New and Used Technology

Our project aims to create an app that allows users to purchase devices from us, or trade in pre-owned devices. We want to bring the digital world closer to meet the needs of our customers. In a day and age where everything is done on the internet, we strive to ensure our customers are not being left behind and have the digital world at their fingertips by offering our products at a wide range of prices with an equally wide range of selection and variety, so there is a product for everyone.

Why TechHaven Matters

As a team, we believe this platform is significant because in addition to being a sales platform, we offer other services which allows users to trade in older devices to reduce the cost of their next purchase, or to be properly disposed of.

Our trade-in option ensures that not only can consumers make some profit, but we ensure that the products are handled in an eco-friendly way, preventing chemical leaks from damage to the battery through the usual garbage disposal means.

Project Postmortem

This postmortem reflects on our development journey, highlighting both challenges and successes we encountered while building TechHaven. We've included insights about our process, architecture decisions, and lessons learned for future projects.

System Architecture

Our project implemented a three-tier architecture consisting of Presentation, Business Logic, and Data layers. We chose this approach to ensure clean separation of concerns and improve testability.

Architecture Diagram

Click to enlarge

What we'll start doing:

More thorough database design planning before implementation, automated UI testing from the beginning, and more frequent code reviews to catch issues early.

What we'll keep doing:

Interface-based design, clear separation of concerns in architecture

What we'll stop doing:

Underestimating UI implementation complexity, postponing database design decisions, and working in isolation on interconnected components.

Iteration 2 Improvement Area

After our iteration 2 retrospective, we identified product image handling as needing improvement. Our approach was to implement a dedicated ProductImagePersistence interface with HSQLDB implementation to better manage product images.

The results were significantly improved image loading performance and a more robust ViewPager implementation that enhanced the user experience when browsing product images.

Lessons Learned

More thorough database design planning before implementation, automated UI testing from the beginning, and more frequent code reviews to catch issues early.

Interface-based design, clear separation of concerns in architecture.
