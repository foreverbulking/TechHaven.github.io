Our Vision

"To create an app that allows users to purchase devices from us, or trade in pre-owned devices, bringing the digital world closer to meet the needs of our customers."

Accessibility

Ensure our customers are not being left behind by offering products at a wide range of prices with an equally wide range of selection and variety.

Customer Satisfaction

Aim for at least 80% of our customers to rate their experience as at least 4/5 stars and recommend us to others.

Intended Users

Our app targets the general public because everyone regardless of age or profession wants access to the internet, and therefore owns and needs technology devices.

Students

Our app is useful to students who may need a computer or tablet to take notes or use for school. We offer affordable options that meet academic requirements.

Day-to-day Users

Our app is also useful to everyday individuals who need a phone or other devices for day-to-day activities, providing reliable technology for communication and productivity.

Smart Shoppers

Our refurbished options could be useful to those who need reliable devices at a discounted price, offering quality technology without the premium price tag.

Major Functionality

Our app provides several key features designed to create a seamless shopping experience for technology products while supporting eco-friendly practices.

Project Postmortem

This postmortem reflects on our development journey, highlighting both challenges and successes we encountered while building TechHaven. We've included insights about our process, architecture decisions, and lessons learned for future projects.

System Architecture

Our project implemented a three-tier architecture consisting of Presentation, Business Logic, and Data layers. We chose this approach to ensure clean separation of concerns and improve testability.

Architecture Diagram

Click to enlarge

What we'll start doing:

More thorough database design planning before implementation, automated UI testing from the beginning, and more frequent code reviews to catch issues early.

What we'll keep doing:

Interface-based design, clear separation of concerns in architecture

What we'll stop doing:

Underestimating UI implementation complexity, postponing database design decisions, and working in isolation on interconnected components.
